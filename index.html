Saint-Maurice Church

The Saint-Maurice Church is a beautiful old Catholic church in the historic center of Lille, Northern France. It has many lovely pieces of art and pretty stained glass, which makes this church simply charming. Its construction began at the end of the 14th century and completed at the end of the 19th century, and it was extended over more than four centuries. Built on top of an old place dedicated to Mars, the roman God of War, this church has an unique orientation and is the only one to be dedicated to Saint Maurice in the North of the France.

Address: 19 Parvis Saint-Maurice, 59800 Lille, France.

5:30 pm

Domaine des Cigognes

Located at 20 minutes from the center of Lille, Domaine des Cigognes is an exceptional place to live warm and friendly moments. The charm of its inner courtyard and the beauty of its 4-hectare park will delight you. This 200-year-old estate was built by a farmer for his Mistress. They say the farmer placed at the top of the chimney a nest & storks (called “Cigognes” in French). Few years ago, the estate has been decorated with stained glass windows and chandeliers. In 2009, new expansion works and modernization have been launched to host receptions within a magical atmosphere!

Address: Departementale 917 / Pont Thibault, 59710 Ennevelin, France.

Ceremony

The wedding ceremony will take place at 3:30 pm in Saint-Maurice Church in Lille. We recommend our guests to come by metro or taxi to the church. It is perfectly accessible by metro, station Gare Lille Flandres. You only need to walk 5 minutes from the station to the church. For our out of town guests, we recommend that you buy a metro pass instead of buying unitary tickets. Transpole, the public transport operator for the Lille Métropole, proposes passes for 1 day, 2 days, 3 days and much more (see the whole proposed passes here). All tickets and passes can be bought from the vending machines in the metro stations. There is no parking dedicated for the church visitors, so we kindly ask our guests that plan to come by their own cars to allow extra time to park in Lille, which might be complicated.

Following the Lebanese ritual, I will arrive at the church with my father who will give me away to Benjamin, the man who will care for me as much as my father did. Then, Benjamin and I will walk down the aisle after all the guests have been seated. In Christian ceremonies, the bride's family sits on the left on the first rows, the groom's on the right on the first rows. Likewise, the bride stands at the left at the altar while the groom stands to her right.

We chose to bless our union within a bi-ritual Christian ceremony: Latin and Maronite. The Maronite church is in full communion with the Pope and the Catholic Church. The Maronite community is mainly concentrated in Lebanon, but also shows presence in Syria, Cyprus and Palestine. The Latin part of our ceremony will be celebrated by father Xavier and the Maronite part will be celebrated by father Jad. Maronite rituals, like the wedding crowning, and some Arabic prayers as well will be added to the Latin ceremony in order to reflect our multicultural - Lebanese and French - fusion. The duration of the ceremony will be around 1 hour and 15 minutes.

After the ceremony, guests might shower Benjamin and me with flower petals when we leave the church. This tradition symbolizes fertility and fruitfulness of the newlyweds.

Dress Code

Our wedding will be celebrated in a glamorous atmosphere. Long or midi dresses for women and suits for men are perfectly adequate for our wedding style. We kindly ask you to avoid white and cream colors. Women may like to change their high heels later in the evening with some more comfortable shoes to better enjoy the party.

The weather in Lille in March is relatively cold and might be rainy. The reception place is indoors and warmed up. Although the church does have heating facilities, it might still feel a little cold inside so do not forget to put on a nice coat!

Top Things in Lille

Lille is a beautiful city in Northern France, it is mostly known for its cold weather and warm citizens. People in Lille mainly speak French, English, and Ch’ti*! During my last 3 years spent in Lille I fell in love with the roads and gastronomy of this city, and with Benjamin too who is born and raised in the region of Lille. We wish you that you appreciate your stay in Lille and we recommend you some activities:

  • Get lost in the old historic part of Lille (Le vieux-Lille).
  • Visit La Citadelle and Palais des Beaux-Arts.
  • Try the Belgian beers, which are stronger than your average beer: Tripel Karmeliet, La Chouffe, Kwak, Chimay bleu, Chimay rouge, Rince Cochon, etc. They can be found in any bar in Lille or even in supermarkets.
  • Try the cheesy plates of Lille, especially the ones made from the “Maroilles” cheese, such as welsh au maroilles (see the plate in the thumbnail that you clicked on to read this section). Be careful, maroilles smells.
  • For those who are not a fan of cheese, try carbonade flamande, which is sweet-sour beef and onion stew made with beer.

Here are some restaurants we recommend: Estaminet Au Vieux de la Vieille , Estaminet la Vieille France , and Le Broc . All these restaurants are available upon reservation. We also recommend waffles at Méert . And finally, do not leave without trying the famous Merveilleux from Merveilleux de Fred.

Besides being a very cool city for chilling, Lille is near the border with Belgium, very close to Paris, few hours from parts of Holland, Luxembourg and Germany. So think about staying a few more days to take advantage of this strategic position of Lille and visit the neighboring cities.
